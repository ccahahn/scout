*This is my additional question—critical for scoping the scout agent.*

The ideal prototype user is:
- **Solo or 1-2 person shop**: Sole grant writer at a small nonprofit ($90K-$500K revenue) OR independent consultant with 1-3 clients
- **One major active project**: Not juggling dozens of grant cycles simultaneously; they have bandwidth to go deep but not wide
- **Paying customer who's underwhelmed**: They subscribed because they heard Instrumentl is great, but they feel like they're drowning in matches and not getting the value they expected. They might be at risk of churning.
- **Not a power user**: They haven't mastered filters, don't use all the features, and feel guilty about it. They want someone (or something) to just *tell them* what's worth their time.
- **Grant browsing cadence matters to them**: They're not always in "active hunting" mode. Sometimes they're just keeping an eye out. They resent getting pinged when they're in a quiet phase.

This user doesn't need more features—they need fewer decisions. The scout agent's job is to act as a trusted colleague who pre-digests the firehose and surfaces only what's genuinely worth a look, in the user's own language.
